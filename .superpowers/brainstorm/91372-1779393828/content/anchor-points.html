Which anchor points per digit?

Component derives all 7 segment centers from these. Pick the minimum set that feels natural to configure.

2 points: bounding box corners

Top-left + bottom-right. Component uses fixed proportions (standard 7-seg geometry) to place all 7 sample points.

⚠ Assumes standard digit proportions — may miss if display is non-standard

3 points: top, middle, right ⭐ Recommended

Center of top segment (a) + center of middle segment (g) + center of top-right segment (b). Gives full geometry: 3 y-levels + left/right x.

✓ Works for any digit proportions. Simple to calibrate.

4 points: top, middle, bottom, right

Adds explicit bottom segment (d) center. No symmetry assumption for vertical height — handles asymmetric digit shapes.

✓ Most accurate. Only 1 extra point over 3-pt.

How derivation works (3-pt example):
Given a=(40,8), g=(40,60), b=(72,34):
→ y_top=8, y_mid=60, y_bot=112 (reflected), x_right=72, x_left=8 (mirrored)
→ All 7 sample points derived. Green dots = derived. ●×7
